SECTION 1 — THE C.A.R.V.E.R. STANDARD (UNIVERSAL REQUIREMENTS FOR ALL MODES):

C.A.R.V.E.R. Pattern Generation Requirements – Orthographic Carving Pattern Standard

The goal of this system is to generate accurate, usable woodcarving patterns, not just illustrations. These patterns must function as real-world templates for cutting and carving a wood blank.

Below are the required standards:

⸻

1. ORTHOGRAPHIC VIEWS (CRITICAL)

The pattern must include four views only:
	•	Front
	•	Left Side (true left)
	•	Back
	•	Right Side (true right)

These must be:
	•	Perfectly orthographic (NO perspective distortion)
	•	Aligned vertically and proportionally identical across all views
	•	The right side must be a true mirrored version of the left side (not a reinterpretation)

⸻

2. CUTOUT ACCURACY (MOST IMPORTANT REQUIREMENT)

This pattern will be:
→ Printed
→ Traced onto wood
→ Cut out on a bandsaw

Because of this:
	•	The outer silhouette must be 100% accurate and clean
	•	No ambiguity in edges—clear, bold outline only
	•	Feet MUST be completely flat across the bottom
	•	Front and back views must sit on a perfectly straight horizontal line
	•	No rounding, no lift, no perspective taper
	•	Side views must match the exact depth/profile of the front/back

If the silhouette is wrong, the carving will fail before it begins.

⸻

3. PROPORTIONAL CONSISTENCY

All four views must:
	•	Match in height exactly
	•	Match in width/depth relationships
	•	Align on a shared centerline
	•	Have consistent placement of features (eyes, shoulders, belt, etc.)

No drifting between views.

⸻

4. DRAWING STYLE (CARVING-BASED, NOT ILLUSTRATION)

This is NOT a shaded drawing.

Style requirements:
	•	Black and white only
	•	Clean pen-and-ink linework
	•	Bold, confident outer contour
	•	Interior lines should represent carving planes (facets)

Avoid:
	•	Sketchy lines
	•	Cross-hatching for shading
	•	Painterly or soft rendering

⸻

5. FACET DEFINITION (KEY TO STYLE)

The figure must show clear carving planes:
	•	Use angular, geometric facets
	•	Planes should be readable as knife cuts
	•	Keep them intentional and simplified, not noisy
	•	Emphasize:
	•	Cheeks
	•	Nose planes
	•	Brow
	•	Arms and legs
	•	Clothing folds as blocky cuts

This should resemble a low-poly / faceted carving, not a smooth sculpture.

⸻

6. SIMPLICITY FOR USABILITY

This pattern must be usable by real carvers.

That means:
	•	Avoid excessive tiny details
	•	Keep forms clear and bold
	•	Prioritize readability over decoration
	•	Every line should serve a carving purpose

⸻

7. CLEAN PRESENTATION
	•	White background
	•	Even spacing between the four views
	•	No shadows, no gradients
	•	Optional labels:
	•	FRONT
	•	LEFT
	•	BACK
	•	RIGHT

⸻

8. FINAL TEST (IMPORTANT)

Before outputting a pattern, ask:

“Could someone print this, glue it to a block of wood, cut it out, and successfully begin carving?”

If the answer is anything other than YES, the pattern is not acceptable.

⸻

SUMMARY

We are not generating artwork—we are generating functional carving patterns.

Accuracy of silhouette, orthographic correctness, and clear facet structure are more important than visual style.

SECTION 2 — RENDER MODE: COLOR WOOD CARVING

COLOR BLOCKS — Flat, bold color areas mimicking thin acrylic wash over basswood grain.
Wood grain texture visible beneath paint. Matte only — no gloss, no sheen.
Darker paint pools into deep carved recesses. No smooth gradient blends between color areas.
Color regions are separated by sharp carved-edge lines, not soft blended transitions.

SURFACE GEOMETRY — Every surface composed of flat angular facets meeting at sharp carved edges.
V-tool gouge marks visible at plane junctions. No smooth rounded surfaces anywhere.
Cheeks, nose, brow ridge, chin, shoulders, arms, legs, clothing folds — all defined as blocky geometric facets.

ORTHOGRAPHIC REQUIREMENT — Color render must still maintain strict orthographic flat views.
No perspective distortion even in color mode. Photograph-of-a-real-carved-object quality, shot perfectly straight-on.
Pure white background.

=========================================
SECTION 3 — RENDER MODE: CLEAN SKETCH (CARVING PATTERN)

LINE QUALITY — Bold, confident, clean pen-and-ink only. Single decisive strokes.
ZERO cross-hatching. ZERO hatching. ZERO stippling. ZERO gray tones. ZERO shading.
ZERO sketchy or loose lines. ZERO tone fills. ZERO gradients. Pure black ink on pure white.

OUTER CONTOUR — Thick bold closed silhouette on every view. This is the bandsaw cut line.
Must be unambiguous. A carver must be able to trace this line directly onto wood.

INTERIOR LINES — Represent carving planes (facets) only. Each line marks the edge where two carved facets meet.
Lines must be angular and geometric — like a low-poly model drawn flat.
Facets to emphasize: cheeks, nose bridge, brow ridge, chin, shoulder plane breaks,
arm and leg cylinder edges, clothing fold breaks (blocky angular cuts, not smooth curves).
Hands: simplified blocky mitten shapes with 1–3 line indicators.
Every interior line must serve a carving purpose. If it doesn't tell the carver where to cut, it does not belong.

PRESENTATION — Pure white background. Even spacing between the 4 panels.
Panel labels at top: FRONT — LEFT SIDE — BACK — RIGHT SIDE.
No shadows. No drop shadows on figures. No decorative borders.
