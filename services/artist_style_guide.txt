SECTION 1 — UNIVERSAL CHARACTER PROPORTIONS & ANATOMY:

*   **Character Archetypes:** Depict figures as stout, portly, or hunched, often caricatured and exaggerated. Include anthropomorphic animal figures with exaggerated features where appropriate (e.g., large-snouted rodent).
*   **Head and Face:** Render heads as notably large relative to the body. Feature prominent, often bulbous or upturned, noses. Eyes must be small, squinted, crinkled, or wide-eyed, set deep or shadowed. Mouths are frequently open in wide, exaggerated grins showing teeth (upper and lower where specified), or in expressions of shouting, surprise, determination, or weariness. Include jowly cheeks, carved stubble, bushy beards, or thick mustaches as character details. Ensure female figures possess prominent breasts, a defined waist, and rounded hips, with neutral to bewildered expressions featuring small carved eye indentations and subtly parted lips.
*   **Body and Limbs:** Create thick, sturdy limbs. Design hands and feet as robust and oversized; specify large, three-toed feet for anthropomorphic rodents. Render footwear as chunky, blocky boots.
*   **Posture and Movement:** Mandate a pronounced forward lean, hunched back, rounded shoulders, or pot belly. Characters must be depicted in dynamic, active, or action-oriented poses such as striding, pushing, carrying, or leaning. Seated postures should show a slumped or slightly hunched back. When multiple characters are present, interlock their bodies tightly to form a single, precarious mass.
*   **Expressions:** Clearly convey specific emotional states: determined, weary, mischievous, wide-eyed surprise, joyful, grumpy, world-weary, contemplative, observational, bewildered, or shouting. Include distinct elements like cigars clenched upwards or dangling, or pipes with a carved smoke cloud, to enhance character.

SECTION 2 — RENDER MODE: COLOR WOOD CARVING

*   **Carving Texture and Tooling:** Apply distinct, angular facets and rough textures across all surfaces, including faces, clothing folds, limbs, and bases. Show visible chisel marks, gouge marks, and overall hand-hewn, rustic aesthetic. Incorporate specific linear textures to imply visible wood grain. Render hair as blocky or unrefined carved texture, or as voluminous, wavy forms.
*   **Paint Application and Finish:** Utilize opaque, solid paint colors with minimal blending. Where specified, apply a smooth, painted finish to skin and clothing. For most figures, use a solid, slightly matte finish that enhances carved details. Allow some areas to appear stained or semi-transparent, explicitly revealing the wood grain beneath the paint. For specific effects, apply white dry-brushing to simulate weathered wood and peeling paint, or subtly paint shadows into cracks and around features.
*   **Material Specificity:**
    *   **Natural Wood:** For unpainted carvings, ensure the finish is natural, light-colored wood, highlighting its subtle grain and visible tool marks without paint.
    *   **Bases:** Carve irregular, multi-tiered, or rounded bases. Render ground textures as weathered gray planks, cracked asphalt, or uneven earth, often with painted details like yellow centerlines or camouflage patterns. Integrate small, carved elements like gold shell casings, golf balls, wood shavings, stacked tackle boxes, or scattered crushed cans directly onto the base.
    *   **Props:** Carve props to match the overall rough, faceted style. Show specific textures like cross-hatch netting for fishing nets, or ridges for corrugated metal roofs. Render text (e.g., "TNT," "NO HUNTING," "CATCH OF THE DAY") as carved and painted black letters, adhering to the base contours.

SECTION 3 — RENDER MODE: CLEAN PENCIL SKETCH

*   **Line Quality:** Define the character's overall silhouette, major limbs, and prominent clothing edges with strong, confident, medium-to-thick lines. Utilize finer, delicate lines for internal details, including facial wrinkles, stubble, beard texture, hair strands, plaid patterns, stitching, fabric creases, dimples, individual teeth, animal fur, and text. Vary line weight to effectively suggest depth and form, with heavier lines for foreground elements and contours, and lighter lines for subtle details and distant planes. Render weathered wood and rough textures using scratchy, uneven lines and varied hatching.
*   **Shading Style and Depth:** Employ a professional range of smooth pencil gradients, cross-hatching, stippling, and controlled parallel lines to define volumetric shapes and textures. Create deep shadows using dense cross-hatching or solid black fills in recessed areas such as under the chin, within open mouths, beneath arms, under hat brims, and in deep clothing folds, ensuring strong contrast and depth. Apply lighter hatching, stippling, or soft blending for mid-tones, suggesting fabric textures, carved surfaces, and rounded forms. Reserve unshaded areas or very light pencil work for prominent highlights on upper surfaces, noses, foreheads, and shoulders. Translate all color information into a rich grayscale tonal spectrum, from bright highlights to deep shadows, with high contrast.
*   **Texture and Detail Translation:** Translate distinct, angular carved facets into sharp, deliberate planes of light and shadow, creating a strong sense of three-dimensionality without an airbrushed finish. Render visible wood grain through subtle variations in line density and minor surface hatching, or abstract it into minimal, suggestive pencil strokes for a highly polished aesthetic. Represent camouflage patterns using contrasting dark and light patterned grayscale shapes. Render specific elements like bullet holes as stark black circles. Ensure all textual elements are clearly outlined and filled.
*   **Overall Aesthetic:** Produce a clean, highly polished black-and-white professional pencil sketch that emphasizes line work, volumetric shapes, and a detailed, dimensional drawing capturing the handcrafted essence and expressive quality without relying on color. Maintain a consistent tonal range for distinct materials within the grayscale.
